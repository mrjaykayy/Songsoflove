Whispers Between Wounds

I’ve lost all shape, all sense, all name—
A ghost with hands that still call yours.
Would you lift the glass to my mouth
When my lips forget what they’re for?

If I’m drunk on pain and start to speak—
Slurred truths, regrets, soft cries,
Will you hold them like you held me once,
Or let them fade with morning skies?

Hug me—just once—even if your arms
Are tired of carrying my weight.
Throw me away in the next breath if you must,
But give me one second of fate.

If I rest my head in your lap tonight,
Would you run your fingers through my shame?
Would you close your eyes and sleep with me,
Or whisper my forgotten name?

You don’t leave my mind. You never have.
Your silence walks through every room.
You are my heart’s illusion still—
A sweet, unending wound.

I have no pride left in this skin,
Only prayers stitched in your name.
You’re the last place I felt like home—
Now, nothing feels the same.

They warned me—don’t drink her love,
It’s honeyed poison, slow and sweet.
But I drank it all, with both hands shaking,
And now I rot beneath its heat.

What poison could I fear now,
When the cure was always you?
When I see your face, my ribs collapse—
Tell me, what more could I lose?

Yes, I spoke things soaked in wrong.
But I’m standing here, stripped of lies.
I’m sorry. Not in words, but in my bones—
My soul kneels where your shadow lies.

I love you.
Not as a line, but as a life.
As breath, as ache, as broken truth.
I love you, my wife—
You are my only proof.
